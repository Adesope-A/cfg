Food in Dublin
Great Restaurants and Cafes

Finding a place to eat?

Are you on a buzz for some flavoursome and Instagram-worthy grub? Let us introduce you to some awesome places, Dublin's famous dining spots.

Sometimes it is difficult to decide where to eat due to a wide variety of choices.

We have listed out some popular restaurants and cafes for your reference.

1. Dublin Pizza Company

The Dublin Pizza Company is placed on Aungier Street, a few doors down from Aungier Danger. They serve authentic delicious pizza that’s cooked right in front of your eyes. Most important is, they are open from 12pm to 3pm and 5pm to midnight every day. They build they polytunnel near Stoneybatter in which they grow their own organic ingredients. Head along to Aungier Street for the tasty pizza!

2. Pang

Dublin’s first rice paper roll restaurant Pang is now open on Kevin Street Lower, Dublin 2 and is bringing the popular food trend in Sydney and Melbourne to our own fair city. Just like sushi, it’s a chilled product that’s super healthy – this is creative nourishment with Vietnamese inspo at its core.

3. Network Cafe

Network Café is all about connecting coffee addicts with quality coffee. The interior design of the café is attractive and gives the space an open feeling. They serve nice and perfectly brewed coffee. They offer a range of beans such as 3FE and Caravan Roastery. Other than coffee, Network Café serves food like pastries. This makes the coffee shop a great breakfast and lunch spot for people to reconnect with friends or their inner self. It is located at Aungier Street.

4. Kaph Cafe

A Paleo-friendly café, Kaph’s menu includes a coconut milk latte or almond milk flat white, as well as sweet treats such as gluten- and dairy-free brownie for those wanting something sweet along with their coffee. A small yet cosy coffee shop, Kaph is positioned on Drury’s Street in Dublin’s City Centre, making it a good resting spot after day of sightseeing or shopping.
